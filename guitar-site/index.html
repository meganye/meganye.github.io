Types of Guitars


Nylon-String Classical


Classical guitars, also known as nylon string or folk guitars, have hollow bodies and wider necks compared to steel string guitars. These guitars also have open pegheads, a stationary loop type bridge, and nylon strings tied at the base. Genres for this type include classical, folk, Latin, and jazz. Fingerpicking is the most common playing style used.



Steel-String Acoustic


With a flat top hollow body, pick guard, and a narrow neck, the steel string acoustic guitar is best suited for folk, country, ragtime, blues, and pop songs. It is recommended to use light gauge bronze strings with ball ends. Using a pick, fingers, or using the thumb and fingers.



Electric


There are two types of electric guitars: solid body and semi-hollow body. Both require amplification to be heard for regular playing. Sound relies heavily on pick-ups and the amplifier. The solid body electric is common used in blues and rock, while the semi-hollow body is used in country, rock, jazz, pop, and blues.
